# Free audit offer — what you actually deliver

This is the deliverable for the free audits during the 30-day validation
phase. Goal: prove the value is real, harvest feedback, convert ~10% to paid.

## The deal

- **They give:** Their handle + primary platform + 1 niche keyword.
  Plus 10 minutes of feedback after reading the report (15-min call OR
  written reply — their choice).
- **You give:** A Markdown report (use the tool's tab 5) with:
  - 10 ranked arbitrage opportunities
  - 1 edge audit (their handle)
  - Cross-platform recommendations
  - 3-5 specific topic+format ideas they can act on this week
- **Time to deliver:** Within 48 hours of them saying yes.

## Why limit to 48 hours?

Speed = signal of competence. A creator who waits a week thinks you're not
prioritizing them. A creator who gets the report in 24h thinks "this person
ships fast."

## What to do AFTER they read it

Send this message exactly 24 hours after delivery:

> Hey — did you get a chance to read it? Two questions while it's fresh:
>
> 1. Which opportunity (if any) feels most actionable for you right now?
> 2. What did the report MISS that you'd want next time?
>
> No agenda, just want to make this thing actually useful.

**This is the conversion moment.** Their answer to Q1 tells you the upsell:

| Their answer | Your upsell |
| --- | --- |
| "I'd want a 90-day plan for opportunity #3" | $2,000 Strategy |
| "I want this every month, things move fast" | $299/mo Retainer |
| "Can you do this for my 3 client accounts?" | $299/mo SaaS Agency |
| "Honestly, I just want the report quarterly" | $500 quarterly audit (4x/yr = $2k) |
| "Cool but not for me right now" | Ask for a referral — see below |

## The referral ask (if they're not buying)

> Totally fair. One ask: do you know 1-2 other creators at your level who
> might find this useful? I'm doing 30 free audits to validate the offering
> and I'd love your intros.

Aim for 2 referrals per "no". A 30-prospect campaign with 30% reply rate and
50% no-with-referral becomes ~60 total prospects by week 3.

## The trap to avoid

DO NOT add features to the free audit because someone asked. The free version
is a teaser. If someone wants a deeper dive, that's the paid tier. Boundaries
prevent the free offer from cannibalizing the paid one.

## Track every audit delivered

In `prospect-tracker.csv`, add columns:
- `audit_delivered_date`
- `feedback_received` (yes/no)
- `feedback_summary` (one line)
- `converted_to_paid` (yes/no, which tier)
- `referrals_given` (count, handles)

Review weekly. After 10 audits, look for the pattern:
- If <20% give feedback -> your audit isn't valuable enough -> improve it
- If <10% convert -> your pricing or pitch is wrong, not the product
- If conversion is fine but referrals are 0 -> you didn't ask hard enough
