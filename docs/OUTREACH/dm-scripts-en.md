# DM scripts — English

Three scripts, in order of strength. Always personalize the FIRST sentence.
Never paste a script without inserting at least one prospect-specific detail.

## Script 1: Cold open with FREE audit offer (default)

> Hey {first_name} — I just saw your {post_reference} hit {engagement_number}
> {reactions/views/comments}. Quick question: have you ever looked at where
> THAT exact topic is getting traction on the platforms you're NOT on?
>
> I built a tool that scans 6 platforms (YouTube, TikTok, X, LinkedIn, Reddit,
> Substack) and finds topics with high demand on one but almost no creators on
> another. Cross-platform arbitrage, basically.
>
> Happy to run it on your niche for free in exchange for 10 minutes of
> feedback. No pitch attached. Want me to send the report?

**Why it works:** specific opener (proves human), curiosity gap (where you're
NOT), free + low-commitment ask, explicit no-pitch promise.

**Common reply:** "Sure, what do you need from me?" -> reply with `free-audit-offer.md`.

## Script 2: Pattern interrupt (use after 3+ ignored DMs to similar people)

> {first_name}, contrarian take I want to test on you:
>
> Every creator tool dashboard tells you what's TRENDING. But by the time it's
> trending, 10,000 other creators are already on it -> margins go to zero.
>
> What if there's a tool that shows you the opposite: topics PROVEN to work
> on one platform but where nobody's published on the platform you care about
> yet?
>
> I built this. Built it for me, but I think it solves a real problem for
> people at your level. Want me to run your niche through it (free) and show
> you what comes out?

**Why it works:** opens with a frame they probably agree with, builds tension,
makes the offer feel like an invitation to validate a hypothesis (not a pitch).

## Script 3: Warm referral (use after first 1-2 paid clients exist)

> {first_name} — {referrer_name} mentioned you might find this interesting.
>
> Last month I ran a content-arbitrage audit for {referrer_name}: scanned 6
> platforms, found 8 topics his audience loves on Substack but where he'd
> hit basically zero LinkedIn supply. He published 2 of them; one got
> {specific_outcome} in 10 days.
>
> Worth 15 minutes of your time? I can run yours and walk you through it.

**Why it works:** social proof + specific outcome + soft ask. This becomes
your default script once you have any results to point to.

## What NEVER to send

- Anything with the words "synergy", "unlock", "elevate", "thought leader"
- "Hope this finds you well" (LinkedIn corporate-speak repels creators)
- Anything over 5 sentences
- A pitch deck attachment unsolicited
- Multiple CTAs ("happy to jump on a call OR send a doc OR...")  -> pick ONE
- "Following up" 24 hours later (use 5-day gap minimum)

## Cadence

- **Day 0:** Initial DM
- **Day 5:** ONE follow-up if no reply: *"Realize you're probably swamped — last
  ping. Free audit offer stands if you ever want it: {one_sentence_recap}."*
- **Day 12:** Move to dead. Don't be that person.

## Volume guidance

- LinkedIn: max 10 connection-invites + DMs per day or you'll get rate-limited
- Twitter/X DMs: max 25/day on a fresh account, 50/day on aged
- Substack: use their built-in comment/DM system, max 5/day per author
- Email: 30-50/day from a warm domain is fine, more needs a sending service

Pace yourself across 7 days. Quality > volume.
