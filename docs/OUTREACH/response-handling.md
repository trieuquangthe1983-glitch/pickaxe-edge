# Response handling — what to say when they reply

The 4 most common replies and your one-message responses to each.

## Reply type 1: "Sure, send it" / "Yes, interested"

**Your response (within 1 hour):**

> Great. Three things I need to send your report:
>
> 1. Your primary platform + handle
> 2. The niche keyword you want me to focus on (one is fine — multi-niche
>    audits are the $500 paid tier)
> 3. Your email (so I can deliver the report PDF/Markdown there — DM
>    attachments are flaky)
>
> Once I have those I deliver within 48 hours.

**Why:** sets expectations, narrows scope (so the free audit doesn't bloat),
moves them out of DM (lower friction for the upsell later).

## Reply type 2: "Tell me more / how does it work?"

**Your response:**

> Three steps:
>
> 1. I run your handle through the tool. It scans your last 90 days of
>    content + scans 6 platforms in your niche.
> 2. I get back ~20 cross-platform arbitrage opportunities, ranked. I take
>    the top 10 + add an edge audit (where you're standing out vs. where
>    you're indistinguishable from peers).
> 3. You get a Markdown report. Takes you 15 min to read. You can act on
>    it immediately or shelve it.
>
> No salesy 1-hour call. Just the report and a follow-up Q. Want me to
> start with your niche?

**Why:** answers the question in 3 bullets, ends with a soft yes/no question
to keep momentum.

## Reply type 3: "What's the catch / How are you making money?"

**Your response (be honest — this is the trust-building moment):**

> Fair question. The catch:
>
> - I'm doing 30 free audits this month to validate the product. ~10% will
>   probably want a paid version ($500 audit, $2k 90-day strategy, or
>   $299/mo monthly briefing). The other 90% I learn from and never bother
>   again.
> - I get your feedback (good and bad) which is more valuable to me right
>   now than $500.
>
> No upsell pressure. Worst case you get a free report and tell me it's
> garbage — that's still useful data for me.

**Why:** transparent about the business model = builds trust. Caps the
downside they imagine ("worst case = free report") = removes friction.

## Reply type 4: "Not for me right now"

**Your response:**

> All good. Quick ask in case I can be useful another way:
>
> Do you know 1-2 creators around your level who might find this useful?
> I'd love an intro. And of course if I can ever help you with anything,
> ping me.

**Why:** referral ask + door open for future. Don't argue. Move on.

## Edge case: They reply YES then ghost after delivery

Send this 5 days after delivery if they never opened/replied:

> Hey — wanted to check in. Did the report land? If you opened it and
> hated it, I genuinely want to know. If you opened it and forgot to reply
> (happens), no worries — happy to walk through any of the 10 opportunities
> over async DM if useful.

If still nothing after 5 more days: log as "dead, do not pursue". Don't take
it personally. Creators are busy.

## Edge case: They give brutal feedback

> "This is useless because X."

Your response:

> Genuinely useful, thanks. Question: if X were fixed, would you pay $500
> for the version that fixes it? Want to make sure I'm building toward a
> real "yes" before I invest in fixing it.

**Why:** brutal feedback either contains real product gaps OR it's a pitch
to do free work. The conditional-paid question separates them.

## Edge case: They want to white-label / resell

> "Can I sell this to my own clients?"

This is the agency tier ($299/mo) appearing organically. Response:

> Yes — that's the Agency tier. $299/mo, unlimited reports under your brand,
> white-label PDFs, multi-client dashboard. Want to do a trial run on one of
> your clients so you can sell-with before subscribing?

NEVER give white-label access for free. The agency tier is the highest-margin
tier and the most defensible business line — protect it.

## Scoring conversations weekly

Once a week, tag every conversation:
- **Hot:** Replied within 24h, asked detail questions, gave email
- **Warm:** Replied within a week, vague but interested
- **Cold:** Replied "maybe later" or after a week
- **Dead:** No reply after follow-up + 7 days

Spend 80% of time on Hot, 20% on Warm. Don't waste energy on Cold and Dead.
